Phone answering services have been the default fallback for UK small businesses for decades. An AI receptionist is the newer alternative. This piece runs both options through the same lens: real cost, response speed, and what you actually get for the money.

You are an electrician in Sheffield. A customer messages your WhatsApp at half seven on a Thursday evening asking about rewiring a kitchen. You are driving home, hands on the wheel. Your phone buzzes twice more before you park. By the time you check the messages, two of the three enquiries have already messaged a competitor.

The standard fix for this problem in the UK has been a telephone answering service. A real person sits in a call centre, picks up calls in your business name, takes a message, and forwards it to you. It costs money, it covers phone calls, and it has been sold to trades and service businesses for thirty years.

The newer alternative is an AI receptionist running on WhatsApp, which answers in seconds, knows your services and rates, and works at two in the morning for the same price as two in the afternoon. The question this piece answers honestly is: which costs less and which actually solves the problem?

1. What Each Option Actually Does

Before comparing prices it is worth being clear about what you are actually buying in each case.

A phone answering service puts a human operator at the end of your diverted phone number. When you cannot pick up, the call diverts, a person answers in your business name, takes a message or follows a script, and sends you a text or email. The better providers can book appointments if you share a calendar. The service typically covers phone calls only. WhatsApp messages, contact form submissions, and SMS enquiries are not in scope unless you pay specifically for those channels.

An AI receptionist sits on your WhatsApp Business number and handles incoming messages automatically. It knows your services, your rates, your postcode coverage, and your hours because you configure it once when you set it up. It replies within seconds, asks qualifying questions, and either books a slot, routes an emergency to your phone, or passes the conversation to you when the customer wants a human. It does not handle traditional phone calls unless you have a missed call to WhatsApp divert set up on your number.

Both solve the same core problem: enquiries that arrive when you cannot personally respond. They solve it via different channels and at different price points.

3. Response Speed and What Customers Actually Experience

This is the dimension most comparison articles skip. Cost per month matters, but what the customer experiences in the first thirty seconds determines whether they stay with you or go to the next result on Google.

With a phone answering service: the caller is diverted to a call centre. There is usually a small wait while an operator picks up, then they introduce themselves in your business name, take the details, and end the call. The message reaches you by text or email, typically within five minutes. If the customer already tried your number once and went to voicemail before the divert kicked in, they may not try again. They certainly did not get an answer to their actual question.

With an AI receptionist on WhatsApp: the customer's message is answered in under five seconds. Not a generic auto-reply that says "we will get back to you." A real response that acknowledges what they asked, asks the right follow-up question, and moves toward booking or triaging the job. From the customer's perspective it looks like a fast, competent business.

Research from Harvard Business Review found that responding to a lead within five minutes makes you seven times more likely to qualify that lead than waiting an hour. After twenty four hours the conversion rate drops to near zero. Plumbing, electrical, and cleaning enquiries are decided in minutes, not days.

The speed gap matters most at evenings and weekends, which is exactly when both options diverge the most. An answering service charges more for evening calls. An AI receptionist charges the same and replies faster.

4. What Gets Handled and What Falls Through

Neither option is a complete replacement for a skilled human. Both have genuine limits worth understanding before you sign up for either.

Phone answering services are strong at:

  • Handling voice-first customers who prefer calling over typing.
  • Following a script if you have written one for complex bookings.
  • Appearing as a human presence on the phone, which some older customers prefer.

Phone answering services fall short at:

  • WhatsApp, which is now the primary channel for under-fifty UK consumers.
  • Knowing your actual services, rates, and coverage area without significant scripting effort.
  • Triaging a genuine emergency vs a routine booking in real time.
  • After-hours coverage at a price that makes sense for a sole trader or small team.

AI receptionists are strong at:

  • WhatsApp enquiries at any hour for a fixed cost.
  • Qualifying the job type, postcode, and urgency automatically.
  • Routing genuine emergencies to your phone immediately using configurable keywords.
  • Handling repeat questions (pricing, availability, what you cover) without any human time.

AI receptionists fall short at:

  • Phone calls directly. You need a missed call divert to WhatsApp to bridge the gap.
  • Customers who specifically want to speak to a human immediately (though the AI hands off when asked).
  • Very complex quote discussions where back-and-forth over a site visit is the only way forward.

For most UK service businesses in 2026, the bigger leak is the WhatsApp message that sits unread for three hours, not the phone call that went to an answering service. The channel has shifted.

5. Which One Is Right for Your Business?

The answer depends on two things: where your enquiries actually come from and what time of day your business is most vulnerable to missing them.

If your customers are primarily over sixty, you are in a traditional trade where people pick up the phone and expect a voice, and your peak enquiry time is nine to five on weekdays, a phone answering service is defensible. You are covering the channel your customers use and the hours when the cost-per-call is at its standard rate.

If your customers are under fifty, send WhatsApp messages from the sofa at half eight in the evening, and would rather get a fast text response than wait to speak to an operator, an AI receptionist covers the actual pattern of your enquiries at a fraction of the cost.

The majority of UK sole traders and small teams in trades, personal services, health and wellness, and local professional services fall into the second group. A plumber in Leeds, a beautician in Bristol, a personal trainer in Glasgow, a locksmith in Birmingham: the enquiries are on WhatsApp, they come in the evenings and weekends, and each missed one is a lost job worth £80 to £400.

There is also a hybrid approach. Keep the answering service for the few customers who still call. Add an AI receptionist for WhatsApp. For many businesses the combined cost of both is still lower than losing three booked jobs a month to unanswered evening messages. 6. If You Are Already Paying for an Answering Service

A lot of small business owners reading this will already have a phone answering service running. Here is the honest position on switching.

Check your contract length first. Most UK answering services lock you in for three or twelve months. If you are mid-contract, note the cancellation date and set a reminder now.

Before you cancel, set up the AI receptionist in parallel for a month. Run both. Track which channel your actual enquiries are arriving on and what the response looks like from both sides. You will know within four weeks whether the WhatsApp volume justifies the switch.

If your call volume is genuinely still significant, keep the answering service for phone calls and add the AI receptionist for WhatsApp only. The combined spend is almost always less than the pure answering service cost once you account for per-call fees and out-of-hours rates.

If your phone enquiry volume has been falling steadily for the past two years and most of your new business now comes through Google Business messages or WhatsApp, the answering service is covering a shrinking channel. The switch makes financial sense.

The Bottom Line

Phone answering services are not bad products. They solve a real problem and they have worked for UK small businesses for a long time. The issue is that the problem has moved. Customers have moved to WhatsApp, enquiries arrive outside business hours, and the per-call cost of a traditional answering service adds up quickly on evening and weekend volume.

An AI receptionist on WhatsApp is not a perfect replacement for every business. If your customers are phone-first, you still need phone cover. But for most UK service businesses under about twenty employees, the AI receptionist covers the higher-volume, higher-cost-to-miss channel for a predictable monthly fee, with no per-call spikes.

The simplest test: look at your last twenty enquiries. Count how many came by phone vs WhatsApp vs other text channels. The answer tells you where to spend your money.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a UK phone answering service cost per month?

Most UK telephone answering services charge between £50 and £250 per month for a small business package. That typically covers a set number of calls and a local number. Per-call overage fees apply once you exceed the package limit, and many providers charge a one-off setup fee of £30 to £100. At typical small business call volumes the all-in monthly cost lands between £100 and £350.

Can an AI receptionist handle phone calls or only WhatsApp messages?

Most AI receptionist products, including Automatyn, operate over WhatsApp rather than replacing a telephone operator. This covers the channel where most UK customers now send enquiries. If you still receive enquiries primarily by phone, look for a product that supports missed call to WhatsApp divert, so callers who do not reach you get an immediate WhatsApp follow-up from the AI within seconds.

Does a phone answering service know my prices and services?

Standard telephone answering services take a name and number and forward it to you. They do not usually know your service area, your rates, or how to triage which jobs are urgent. Premium services offer a scripted call flow but they charge significantly more. An AI receptionist that you configure yourself knows your exact services, rates, postcodes, and emergency keywords from the start.

What happens at 2am if a customer messages?

An AI receptionist replies within seconds regardless of the time. Most phone answering services do offer out-of-hours cover but at a higher per-call rate or on a separate overnight package. For trades where genuine emergencies happen outside business hours, burst-pipe or boiler failures for example, the speed of that first response is the difference between winning the job or losing it to the next number in Google.

Is an AI receptionist suitable for a very small UK business?

Yes. AI receptionists are better suited to small businesses than phone answering services in most cases, because the cost is fixed rather than per-call, and the product works whether you receive three messages a month or three hundred. A sole trader who handles their own bookings but cannot always answer instantly is the ideal user. The setup is under ten minutes and there is usually a free tier to start on.
